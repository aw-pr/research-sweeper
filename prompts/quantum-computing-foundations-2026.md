# Research Brief: Quantum Computing — Foundations, Theory, and Engineering Realities 2024–2026

## Topic string (paste into --topic)

```
Quantum computing fundamentals: theory, qubit hardware approaches, decoherence, error correction, algorithmic complexity, and developer programming models — April 2024 to April 2026
```

## Sub-questions for synthesis to address

**Quantum mechanics foundations**
- What are superposition, entanglement, and interference, and how does each contribute to quantum computational advantage?
- How does quantum measurement collapse work and why does it constrain algorithm design?
- What is quantum speedup, and which complexity classes (BQP, QMA) define what quantum computers can and cannot do faster?
- What are the theoretical limits — problems quantum computers provably cannot solve faster than classical?

**Qubit hardware approaches**
- What are the main qubit modalities (superconducting, trapped ion, photonic, neutral atom, topological) and what are the engineering trade-offs of each?
- How do gate fidelity, coherence time, and connectivity topology differ across hardware types?
- Which hardware approaches are leading in qubit count vs. quality, and why does that distinction matter?
- What is the state of topological qubits (e.g. Microsoft's approach) — genuine progress or still largely theoretical?

**Decoherence and error correction**
- What causes decoherence and what engineering techniques exist to extend coherence time?
- How do quantum error correction codes (surface codes, stabiliser codes) work conceptually, and what qubit overhead do they require?
- What is the error threshold theorem and what physical error rates need to be achieved for fault tolerance?
- Where does the field stand on the road to logical qubits — how many physical qubits per reliable logical qubit today?

**Quantum algorithms and complexity**
- Which algorithms (Shor, Grover, VQE, QAOA, HHL) are most significant, and what hardware scale do they require to outperform classical?
- What is quantum annealing and how does it differ from gate-based quantum computing?
- Are variational quantum algorithms (VQAs) delivering on their near-term promise or hitting fundamental noise barriers?
- What does post-quantum cryptography readiness look like, and how soon does Shor's algorithm become a realistic threat?

**Programming models and developer interfaces**
- How do circuit-level languages (Qiskit, Cirq, Quil) work, and what abstractions sit above them?
- Are higher-level domain-specific languages or compilers emerging that hide qubit-level detail?
- How do quantum cloud platforms (IBM Quantum, Azure Quantum, AWS Braket, IonQ Cloud) differ in access model and capability?
- What does a software engineer need to understand to write and run a meaningful quantum program today?
