# Research Brief: Quantum Computing Advances — State of the Field 2025–2026

## Topic string (paste into --topic)

```
Quantum computing advances, quantum advantage benchmarks, real-world applications, and programming interfaces for business and consumer use — April 2025 to April 2026
```

## Sub-questions for synthesis to address

**Quantum advantage and milestones**
- Has quantum advantage (superiority on specific tasks) been demonstrated beyond narrow toy problems, and by whom?
- Which problem classes (optimisation, simulation, cryptography, ML) show the most credible near-term advantage claims?
- How are error correction and qubit fidelity advances changing the threshold for useful quantum computation?
- What is the realistic timeline to fault-tolerant quantum computing?

**Real-world applications**
- Which industries (pharma, finance, logistics, materials science) have run production or pilot quantum workloads in 2025–2026?
- What are the most credible near-term commercial use cases, and what scale of quantum hardware do they require?
- Are hybrid classical-quantum approaches delivering measurable value today, or mainly positioning?

**Programming interfaces and developer ecosystem**
- What SDKs, frameworks, and cloud APIs (Qiskit, Cirq, Azure Quantum, AWS Braket, etc.) are actively used or newly released?
- How accessible are these tools to software engineers without deep physics backgrounds?
- Are high-level or domain-specific abstractions emerging that hide qubit-level complexity?

**Hype vs near-term reality**
- Where is the gap largest between vendor claims and independently verified results?
- What do independent analysts and academic critics say about the 2–5 year commercial outlook?
- What would need to be true for quantum to become a general-purpose tool for businesses by 2030?
